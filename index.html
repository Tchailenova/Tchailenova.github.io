Try things in 3 phases:

  1. To get past the fear of newness
      2. • Embrace the cringe of a thing done badly
  2. To learn how to do it
      3. • Reject perfectionism, just do the thing & absorb as much as you can
  3. To figure out whether you like it or not
      4. • Seek your joy without prejudice

Inspired by Virgil Thomson

The next three nuggets of wisdom come from a super-successful artistic creator with many years of experience, sourced from a chatty interview podcast with two super-insightful wise guys.
Attribution links point to video timestamps.

Sometimes, full commitment to a stupid idea comes out with some of the best [results] ... If you half-commit to a stupid idea, then it's bad - the whole thing's just rubbish. Whereas if you fully commit to a stupid idea, then it ends up being good.
-Mumbo Jumbo as a guest on the Imp & Skizz Podcast

I genuinely believe that all humans are absolutely super creative and I think sometimes people get caught up in maybe the aesthetics of a creative person [...], or the idea that you have to [be in a certain place] [...], I don't think that's the case at all. Everyone has the capacity to be creative, and I think sharing that actually the creative process does involve pain helps people realize that they are capable of creativity. This is the universal experience: that creativity does come with some pain.
-Mumbo Jumbo as a guest on the Imp & Skizz Podcast

The universal experience [with creativity] is doubting yourself, and struggling with it, and sitting with it, and then progressing, & getting better.
-Mumbo Jumbo as a guest on the Imp & Skizz Podcast

If you know the enemy and know yourself, you need not fear the result of a hundred battles. If you know yourself but not the enemy, for every victory gained you will also suffer a defeat. If you know neither the enemy nor yourself, you will succumb in every battle.
-Sun Tzu, The Art of War

You can't cling to a mistake simply because you spent a lot of time making it.
